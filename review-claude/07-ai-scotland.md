# Review: AI Scotland

## Summary

AI Scotland is the national delivery programme for the strategy, led by the Scottish Government with The Data Lab, ScotlandIS, and enterprise agencies. Its first priority is a renewed SME AI Adoption Programme. An Expert Advisory Board will oversee AI Scotland's work, and the programme's long-term organisational model (cluster management organisation or non-profit company) will be determined during the first year.

## Key Points

- AI Scotland is a consortium-led national programme fulfilling a Programme for Government 2025-2026 commitment.
- Operates across the "triple helix" of business, academia, and the public sector.
- First action: expanded AI Adoption Programme for SMEs, building on a 2025 pilot supported by nearly £1 million of public funding.
- Expert Advisory Board to be established for accountability and strategic direction.
- Long-term organisational model (CMO or NPC) to be determined via a business case in year one.

## Observations

- **Clarity** -- The section is clear on what AI Scotland is and who is involved. However, the relationship between AI Scotland and the ten key actions is ambiguous. Action 1 says "position AI Scotland as the national flagship programme" -- this section describes it as a "national transformation programme." Is AI Scotland responsible for delivering all ten actions, or just some? The boundary of its remit is not defined.
- **Measurability** -- The only concrete figure is "nearly £1 million" for the original 2025 SME programme. No budget is given for the expanded programme, AI Scotland itself, or the Expert Advisory Board. The section says AI Scotland will "coordinate and amplify" efforts but sets no targets for what coordination should achieve.
- **Gaps**:
  - **No timeline for the Expert Advisory Board** beyond "an early action." When will it be constituted? Who selects its members?
  - **The organisational model is deferred.** Spending the first year deciding how to structure the delivery body means the delivery body may not be fully operational until year two. For a strategy with March 2027 action deadlines, this is a risk.
  - **No mention of how AI Scotland relates to existing bodies.** Scotland already has The Data Lab, ScotlandIS, Scottish Enterprise, Highlands and Islands Enterprise, South of Scotland Enterprise, and the Scottish National Investment Bank all operating in adjacent spaces. How AI Scotland avoids duplicating or conflicting with these bodies is not addressed.
- **Feasibility** -- The "nearly £1 million" for the original SME adoption programme is modest. If the expanded programme is to reach a meaningful proportion of Scotland's ~360,000 SMEs, it will need significantly more funding. The section does not indicate the scale of ambition or resources.
- **Internal consistency** -- The consortium partners (The Data Lab, ScotlandIS, enterprise agencies) are all tech-sector and enterprise-focused. The foreword's emphasis on Fair Work and the strategy's commitment to engaging trade unions, community groups, and the public sector are not reflected in AI Scotland's governance or delivery structure.
