# Review: Endnotes

**Light review**

55 endnotes are listed, referencing government publications, university centres, industry reports, and policy frameworks.

## Notable observations

- **No hyperlinks.** The original PDF contained clickable links; the markdown version lists them as plain text titles. This is a known limitation of the transcription process, not a strategy issue.
- **Some references are vague.** For example, endnote 43 ("According to data provided by Scottish Enterprise") gives no publication, date, or way to verify the claim. Endnote 12 and 28 note reports were "commissioned by Scottish Enterprise, used with permission" but are not publicly available, limiting transparency.
- **Duplicate source.** Endnotes 36 and 30 reference the same source (Artificial Intelligence -- Business Insights and Conditions in Scotland, wave 147).
- **No international comparisons cited.** For a strategy that positions Scotland globally, the references are almost entirely domestic (Scottish Government, UKRI, Scottish Enterprise). The only international references are OECD AI principles (31), the EU AI Act (32), and a McKinsey report (39).
