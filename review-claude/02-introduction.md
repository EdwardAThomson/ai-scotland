# Review: Introduction

## Summary

The introduction frames AI as both a disruptor and an opportunity, and positions this strategy as Scotland's plan to harness AI responsibly. It introduces the "AI Stack" as the strategy's organising model and commits to iterative progress reporting and ongoing consultation.

## Key Points

- The strategy aims for responsible AI use delivering sustainable growth and stronger public services.
- The "AI Stack" model structures the strategy into layers requiring action.
- The government commits to publishing regular progress reports and iterating the action plan.
- The strategy was developed through consultation with the Scottish Technology Council, the Data Lab, Prosper, ScotlandIS, SCVO, and the University of Glasgow.
- Two supporting quotes from academia (University of Edinburgh) and industry (Gigged.AI) emphasise Scotland's research strengths and the need for greater global visibility.

## Observations

- **Clarity** -- The AI Stack is introduced as a "new model" with "layers" but is not explained here. The reader is told to expect it but given no preview of what the layers are or why this framing was chosen over alternatives. This is a missed opportunity to orient the reader.
- **Measurability** -- The commitment to "regular reports on progress" is welcome but undefined. No frequency, format, or accountability mechanism is specified. "Regular" could mean annually or never.
- **Gaps** -- The consultation partners listed are predominantly tech-sector and academic. There is no mention of trade unions, healthcare bodies, local government, or citizen groups in the consultation process, despite the strategy's emphasis on public services and Fair Work. The quotes are exclusively from tech/academic voices, reinforcing this imbalance.
- **Internal consistency** -- The introduction promises the strategy will show "how we will keep pace with, and prosper as a result of, rapidly changing global technologies and markets." This is a strong claim that the subsequent sections will need to substantiate with concrete mechanisms, not just aspirations.
