# Review: Glossary of Terms

**Light review**

The glossary defines 22 terms. Definitions are generally clear and accessible to a non-technical reader.

## Notable observations

- **No definition of "AI" itself.** The strategy's central subject is not defined in the glossary. The introduction uses phrases like "reshaping economies" and the foreword mentions "machine learning", but the document never pins down what it means by AI. The glossary defines Machine Learning but treats it as a subset ("one particular form of AI") without defining the parent term.
- **"Artificial General Intelligence" (AGI) is absent.** Given that AGI discourse is shaping global AI policy and public concern, its omission is noticeable -- even if only to explain why the strategy does or doesn't address it.
- **"Fair Work" is not defined** despite being a recurring commitment throughout the strategy. Endnote 38 links to an introduction, but a glossary entry would help readers unfamiliar with this Scottish Government framework.
- **"AI Stack" is absent.** This is the strategy's core structural metaphor (8 layers), yet it is not defined in the glossary.
- **Some definitions are inconsistent in style.** Most define the term in third person ("A semiconductor is..."), but a few shift to second person or use "refers to" phrasing. Minor, but noticeable in a formal document.
