# Review: Purpose & Outcomes

## Summary

A single-page section that defines the strategy's overarching purpose and sets out eight outcome statements grouped under four headings: People & Skills, Companies & Innovation, Infrastructure, and Data & Regulation. These outcomes are intended to describe "what success looks like" by 2031.

## Key Points

- Purpose: "to harness the potential of AI to drive responsible and inclusive growth across our economy and make a positive difference at every level of society."
- Eight outcomes across four groupings, targeting 2031.
- The four groupings map loosely onto the eight AI Stack layers that follow, though the mapping is not made explicit.

## Observations

- **Clarity** -- The purpose statement is broad but serviceable. The outcomes, however, are vague. "Equality of access based on widespread literacy" -- what does widespread mean? "Strong technology clusters" -- strong by what measure? These read as aspirations rather than outcomes. An outcome should describe a measurable end state; most of these describe a direction of travel.
- **Measurability** -- This is the section's central weakness. None of the eight outcomes includes a target, threshold, or metric. Compare "skilled and adaptable workforce enabled by accessible learning pathways" with something like "50,000 workers completing AI skills training by 2031." The current phrasing makes it impossible to assess whether the strategy has succeeded or failed in 2031. The government could claim success on every outcome without demonstrating meaningful change.
- **Gaps**:
  - **No baseline.** If these are outcomes for 2031, where is Scotland starting from? Without a baseline, progress cannot be measured.
  - **No mention of environmental sustainability** as an outcome, despite the Case for Change acknowledging energy and infrastructure pressures.
  - **Fair Work is absent again.** The People & Skills outcomes mention "skilled and adaptable workforce" but not job quality, worker protections, or Fair Work principles -- despite the foreword making this a central commitment.
  - **No outcome for public trust.** The strategy repeatedly emphasises the importance of public trust in AI, yet there is no outcome that measures or targets it.
- **Internal consistency** -- The four outcome groupings do not align neatly with the eight AI Stack layers that structure the rest of the strategy. For example, "Users" (Layer 1) has no clear outcome, and "Semiconductors" (Layer 6) sits awkwardly under the Infrastructure heading. The relationship between these outcomes and the subsequent action plan is left for the reader to infer.
