# Review: Foreword

## Summary

The Deputy First Minister and the Minister for Business and Employment introduce Scotland's AI strategy by framing AI as an opportunity to continue Scotland's tradition of innovation. The foreword commits to responsible AI adoption that benefits everyone, while acknowledging public concerns about jobs, fairness, and transparency.

## Key Points

- Scotland has a historical tradition of innovation (Mary Somerville, John Napier) that provides a foundation for AI leadership.
- The government's ambition is to boost the economy, close the productivity gap, drive innovation, and improve public services through AI.
- AI must be guided by the Scottish Government's commitment to Fair Work.
- Progress and safeguards should go hand in hand.

## Observations

- **Clarity** -- The foreword is well written and accessible. It sets a clear tone for the strategy.
- **Measurability** -- As a foreword, it is naturally aspirational rather than specific. Phrases like "secure the benefits of AI for everyone" and "close the productivity gap" are broad commitments that the strategy body will need to make concrete. Whether it does so is the key question for later sections.
- **Gaps** -- The foreword names economic growth, public services, health, and education as target areas, but does not mention the environment, defence, or creative industries. Energy and environmental concerns around AI infrastructure are a significant theme globally; their absence from the framing is notable.
- **Internal consistency** -- The foreword positions Fair Work as central to the AI approach. This sets an expectation that workforce protections and trade union engagement will feature prominently in the strategy. Worth tracking whether this is followed through.
