# Review: Layer 1 - Users

## Summary

The Users layer focuses on public trust, AI literacy, and citizen engagement with AI-enabled services. It sets four outcomes for 2031 (informed citizens, inclusive AI literacy, prepared young people, trusted public services) and six actions for 2027, centring on an engagement programme, an Expert Advisory Board, and an AI framework for health and social care. A case study on Citizens Advice Scotland's AI-enabled safeguarding tool illustrates the approach.

## Key Points

- Citizens' confidence in AI varies, particularly among those with lower digital access and skills.
- The Scottish Approach to Service Design will guide a human-centred approach to AI in public services.
- Six actions by 2027: Expert Advisory Board, nationwide engagement programme, health/social care AI framework, AI literacy materials, public sector AI visibility, and public sector AI governance.
- Four 2031 outcomes: informed citizens, inclusive AI literacy, prepared young people, trusted AI in public services.
- Case study: Citizens Advice Scotland's AI triage tool handled 35,000 cases in 2024, with 70% requiring urgent intervention, demonstrating AI augmenting (not replacing) human judgement.

## Observations

- **Clarity** -- The case study is excellent -- specific, measurable, and grounded. It demonstrates exactly the kind of responsible AI adoption the strategy advocates. The rest of the section, however, is much vaguer. "Promote materials to ensure an understanding of AI" could mean anything from a leaflet campaign to a national curriculum change.
- **Measurability** -- The 2031 outcomes repeat the pattern from section 05: aspirational statements with no metrics. "Widespread literacy and confidence" is not measurable. How many people? Measured how? The case study shows that measurable outcomes are possible (35,000 cases, 75% with health conditions, faster escalation) -- the strategy's own actions should aspire to the same specificity.
- **Gaps**:
  - **"Prepared young people" has no action to deliver it.** The outcome says "education and skills provision equip young people" but none of the six 2027 actions address schools, colleges, or curricula. This outcome has no delivery mechanism.
  - **No mention of accessibility.** For a section about inclusive AI literacy, there is no mention of accessibility for disabled users, non-English speakers, or those without internet access.
  - **No mention of how citizens can challenge AI decisions.** The section says people should understand "what safeguards apply" but does not describe a right to appeal, a complaints process, or a human alternative when AI is involved in public service decisions.
- **Internal consistency** -- Three of the six actions repeat verbatim from the ten key actions in section 06 (Expert Advisory Board, engagement programme, health/social care framework). This means the Users layer introduces only three new actions beyond what was already committed. The section is thinner than it appears.
