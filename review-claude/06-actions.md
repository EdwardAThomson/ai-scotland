# Review: Actions

## Summary

A single page listing ten key actions to be completed before the end of March 2027. These are the strategy's early deliverables, framed as making "significant early progress" towards the outcomes. They span governance (AI Scotland, Expert Advisory Board), public engagement, health, SME adoption, skills, scale-ups, public services, infrastructure, and data.

## Key Points

1. Position AI Scotland as the national flagship programme.
2. Appoint AI Industry Champions across sectors and regions, reporting to an Expert Advisory Board.
3. Launch a nationwide engagement programme for public trust.
4. Implement a framework for safe, ethical AI use in health and social care.
5. Roll out a national AI adoption programme for SMEs, including an AI Leadership Academy.
6. Establish a Future Jobs Panel for workforce impact and skills planning.
7. Pilot an AI Scale-up Accelerator.
8. Launch an innovation programme applying AI to public service delivery.
9. Promote Scotland as a centre for green data centres; maximise the Lanarkshire AI Growth Zone.
10. Launch a data matchmaking pilot for trusted public-sector datasets.

## Observations

- **Clarity** -- The actions are clearly written and more concrete than the outcomes. However, most are "launch", "establish", or "pilot" actions -- they create structures and programmes rather than delivering tangible results. By March 2027, Scotland will have panels, academies, programmes, and pilots, but the actions do not specify what those programmes will deliver.
- **Measurability** -- While the deadline (March 2027) is welcome, success criteria are absent. What does it mean to "position AI Scotland as the national flagship programme"? How many SMEs should the adoption programme reach? How many companies should the Scale-up Accelerator connect? Without targets, these actions can be ticked off simply by launching something, regardless of impact.
- **Gaps**:
  - **No budget or funding commitments.** Not a single action includes a cost, funding source, or resource allocation. An AI Leadership Academy, a Scale-up Accelerator, a nationwide engagement programme, and a data matchmaking pilot all require funding. Where does it come from?
  - **No action on education or schools.** The strategy targets workforce skills (action 6) and SME adoption (action 5) but nothing addresses AI in schools, colleges, or university curricula -- the longer-term skills pipeline.
  - **No action on regulation.** Despite regulation being one of the eight AI Stack layers, none of the ten actions addresses Scotland's regulatory approach or stance.
  - **Creative industries, space, and financial services are absent** from the actions despite being highlighted as priority sectors in Foundations.
- **Feasibility** -- Ten actions in 12 months (from publication in March 2026 to the March 2027 deadline) is ambitious, particularly given that several require establishing new bodies (Expert Advisory Board, Future Jobs Panel) that will need recruitment, governance, and setup time before they can function. The risk is that these are announced by March 2027 but not yet operational.
- **Internal consistency** -- The actions map partially to the outcomes but the relationship is not explicit. There is no traceability from outcomes to actions -- the reader must infer which actions serve which outcomes. Action 4 (health and social care framework) is notably specific to one sector, while other priority sectors from Foundations receive no dedicated action.
