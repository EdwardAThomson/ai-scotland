### 1. Summary

This section distils the strategy's broader ambitions into a sharp, immediate focus, detailing ten specific, time-bound actions to be delivered by March 2027. These actions cover institutional setups (like establishing "AI Scotland" and an Expert Advisory Board) through to programmatic roll-outs (such as an SME adoption program, a scale-up accelerator, and a data matchmaking pilot).

### 2. Key Points

- Create "AI Scotland" as the flagship national delivery programme.
- Appoint AI Industry Champions reporting to an independent Expert Advisory Board.
- Launch a nationwide public engagement programme and establish a Future Jobs Panel.
- Roll out an SME AI adoption programme (including a Leadership Academy) and an AI Scale-up Accelerator.
- Implement frameworks for AI in health/social care and launch an innovation programme for public services.
- Promote Scotland as a green data center hub (focusing on Lanarkshire) and launch a public dataset matchmaking pilot.

### 3. Observations

- **Measurability** -- The commitment to complete these ten highly specific actions "before the end of March 2027" provides an excellent, rigid timeline for strict governmental accountability.
- **Feasibility** -- Delivering ten substantial national initiatives in essentially a single year (assuming a March 2026 launch) indicates an extremely aggressive public sector timeline. This raises questions about delivery feasibility without immense dedicated resourcing.
- **Internal consistency** -- The ten actions map seamlessly back to the four outcome pillars defined in the previous section, demonstrating strong strategic cohesion.
