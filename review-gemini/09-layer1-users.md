### 1. Summary

Layer 1 addresses the primary interface point between the strategy and the public—the citizens interacting with AI in public services and daily life. It aims to foster a digitally literate, informed populace capable of engaging safely with AI, whilst mandating that public services adopt a human-centric, transparent approach to algorithmically-assisted decision making.

### 2. Key Points

- Emphasises the necessity of AI literacy so citizens understand when AI is being used in decisions affecting them.
- Commits to a nationwide engagement programme specifically to listen to public concerns and cultivate trust.
- Prioritises a "human and user-centred approach" utilizing the Scottish Approach to Service Design to ensure AI augments, rather than replaces, human expertise.
- Commits to establishing an independent Expert Advisory Board to verify that AI applications uphold Scotland's ethical and values-led standards.

### 3. Observations

- **Clarity** -- The section is highly articulate in framing the citizen not merely as a passive data point, but as an active participant who requires understanding and rights.
- **Measurability** -- Actions such as "promote materials" or "work with public sector bodies to increase visibility" are somewhat vague and will be difficult to measure precisely without firm quantitative targets.
- **Internal consistency** -- Logically builds upon predecessor frameworks (Digital Strategy for Scotland, 2021 AI Strategy), ensuring administrative continuity rather than restarting policy from scratch.
