### 1. Summary

Layer 4 addresses a persistent "translation gap" where Scotland's world-leading academic AI research frequently fails to convert into proportionate domestic commercial impact. The strategy seeks to overhaul this dynamic by piloting a "Venture Creator" for university commercialisation, strengthening cluster development, and proactively leveraging public sector procurement to stimulate demand for homegrown innovation.

### 2. Key Points

- Candidly acknowledges that excellent academic research remains significantly underused in commercial applications.
- Commits to piloting a novel "Venture Creator" approach to streamline and radically overhaul university commercialisation methods.
- Commits to establishing a national cluster scheme to align AI as an enabling technology across various industries.
- Aims to utilize the public sector proactively, using its purchasing power as a "reference market" for new Scottish AI products.

### 3. Observations

- **Clarity** -- The frank admission of a commercialisation deficit, despite genuine research excellence, is a refreshingly realistic starting point for a policy document.
- **Feasibility** -- Radically altering university commercialisation processes (the "Venture Creator" model) often requires navigating deep-seated institutional inertia, intense academic IP complexities, and risk-averse university administrations.
- **Internal consistency** -- Harnessing public sector procurement to act as a domestic launchpad for innovation ties flawlessly into the objectives of Layer 3 (growing local companies) and Layer 1 (improving public services).
