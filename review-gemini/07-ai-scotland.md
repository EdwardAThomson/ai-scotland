### 1. Summary

This section elaborates on "AI Scotland", establishing it as the unified Scottish Government transformation programme tasked with coordinating and executing the strategy's actions. It outlines its collaborative governance structure, establishes an independent Expert Advisory Board, and outlines plans to define a permanent, formalized long-term organizational model by the end of its first year.

### 2. Key Points

- "AI Scotland" is unequivocally the primary delivery vehicle, led by a consortium including the Scottish Government, The Data Lab, ScotlandIS, and enterprise agencies.
- Identifies an immediate priority of launching a renewed, publicly funded SME AI Adoption Programme.
- Commits to an Expert Advisory Board featuring industry champions and technical experts to provide evaluation and strategic guidance.
- Sets a goal for the first year to develop a comprehensive business case determining AI Scotland's permanent operational model (with options including a cluster management organisation or non-profit company).

### 3. Observations

- **Clarity** -- The section is highly effective at defining exactly *who* will be leading the execution of the strategy, resolving ambiguities common in high-level policy documents.
- **Feasibility** -- Utilizing an existing consortium model (tapping into entities like Data Lab and ScotlandIS) significantly increases the feasibility of rapid deployment, as it bypasses the delays of legislating a new public body from scratch.
- **Measurability** -- The commitment to finalize the target operating model via a business case in year one is a clear, measurable administrative milestone.
