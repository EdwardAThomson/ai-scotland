### 1. Summary

Layer 7 assesses "Data" as the cross-cutting, foundational fuel necessary for the entire AI ecosystem. It candidly diagnoses the current landscape of public sector data as overly fragmented and inaccessible, and outlines coordinated plans to mature institutional governance, standardise secure access, and fully unlock high-quality datasets for both public transformation and private AI research.

### 2. Key Points

- Identifies a critical roadblock: public sector data currently remains fragmented, slow to access, and is under-realising its vast value as a national asset.
- Warns that lacking streamlined, secure data access risks causing Scotland to fall behind in research competitiveness.
- Commits to launching an immediate, practical data matchmaking pilot to connect organisations with trusted public datasets.
- Establishes a joint leadership collaborative group with Local Government to force a more coordinated, unified data approach across administrative borders.

### 3. Observations

- **Clarity** -- Openly and explicitly diagnosing data fragmentation as a severe, systemic risk makes the absolute necessity of rapid policy intervention very clear and justified.
- **Measurability** -- A "data matchmaking pilot" is a highly tangible short-term action; its throughput and user satisfaction ratings will easily yield assessable outcomes.
- **Feasibility** -- Historically, overcoming deeply entrenched data silos across multifarious independent local governments and NHS health boards is notoriously difficult, both technically and culturally. Delivering this will require immense political capital.
