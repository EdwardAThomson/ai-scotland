### 1. Summary

This concluding section catalogues the primary macro-risks of AI deployment and formally maps them against the strategy's mitigating actions. It establishes an ongoing commitment to preemptive impact assessments (covering equality, privacy, and environmental sustainability) to ensure that the rapid scaling of AI adheres strictly to Scotland's legal obligations and civic values.

### 2. Key Points

- Explicitly catalogues key deployment risks: Privacy/Data Protection, Workforce Impacts, Environmental/Energy capacity pressures, Gender Inequality, Sovereign Infrastructure dependencies, and Sectoral disruption.
- Pledges to mandate early-stage impact assessments regarding equality, human rights, and environmental sustainability for all new AI policies.
- Commits to pursuing continuous, active engagement with trade unions and community groups on rapidly emerging workforce impacts.

### 3. Observations

- **Clarity** -- Utilizing a tabular format that maps specific listed risks directly to their corresponding mitigating outcomes provides highly transparent, scannable governance documentation.
- **Gaps** -- While it explicitly identifies "Gender Inequality" concerning the reproduction of biases in datasets, it surprisingly omits broader mention of racial or socioeconomic algorithmic biases, which are equally vital components of the global AI ethics discourse.
- **Measurability** -- While the mitigations sound appropriate, it remains largely unclear how their success will be quantified. For instance, there are no specific threshold metrics defining what constitutes successful mitigation of "Workforce Impacts".
