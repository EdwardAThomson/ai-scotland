### 1. Summary

Layer 2 tackles the dual challenge of low business integration and shifting workforce demands. It aims to actively accelerate AI adoption among SMEs and public services, while simultaneously launching large-scale workforce upskilling initiatives to preserve job quality, alleviate skills shortages, and adapt to the AI economy in alignment with Fair Work principles.

### 2. Key Points

- Identifies a severe adoption gap: despite rising AI job postings, roughly 61.9% of SMEs report not actively using AI technologies.
- Commits a £1 million SME AI Adoption Programme offering practical grants, roadshows, and technical support.
- Pledges to launch a new AI Leadership Academy and an overarching AI readiness tool suitable for SMEs and public bodies.
- Commits to establishing a Future Jobs Panel designed to assess impacts and guide national skills planning.

### 3. Observations

- **Measurability** -- The commitment of £1 million in funding and the target of over 500 SMEs engaged provide highly concrete, measurable program KPIs.
- **Feasibility** -- While the £1 million investment is a positive catalyst, spreading this sum across a national SME business base is relatively modest, which may limit the depth of sustained "hands-on assistance" available to individual firms.
- **Internal consistency** -- This section directly addresses the harsh reality outlined in the "Case for Change" (that domestic business adoption is currently too low to secure the projected GDP boosts).
