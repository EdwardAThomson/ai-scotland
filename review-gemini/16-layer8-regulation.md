### 1. Summary

Layer 8 defines the overarching regulatory environment necessary to instil confidence. It prioritises a principles-based approach explicitly aligned with the OECD and the EU AI Act to preserve Scotland's market access. Crucially, it acknowledges that while innovating via regulatory "sandboxes", providing domestic business certainty heavily relies on advocating for compatible, statutory regulatory frameworks at the overarching UK Government level.

### 2. Key Points

- Commits to an AI regulatory framework deeply aligned with OECD principles and international best practice (specifically citing the EU AI Act).
- Acknowledges that both UK and Scottish regulatory environments must collaborate closely to prevent destructive divergence.
- Commits to advocating for a UK Government approach to AI regulation that formalizes OECD values on a statutory footing.
- Highlights the deployment of regulatory "sandboxes" (like the FCA's) to test sector-specific AI innovations safely against compliance rules.

### 3. Observations

- **Feasibility** -- The strategy's heavy reliance on "advocating" for a specific UK Government statutory approach highlights the reality that Scotland's regulatory autonomy regarding sweeping tech regulation is constitutionally constrained.
- **Clarity** -- The strategic economic intention is unmistakably clear: tight regulatory alignment with Europe (via the EU AI Act) is viewed as a strict economic necessity for maintaining export market access.
- **Internal consistency** -- This section functionally completes the "chain of trust" motif introduced in the foreword, providing the formal, structural guardrails conceptually required to deliver the "Responsible AI" promised throughout the entire strategy.
