### 1. Summary

Layer 5 recognizes that the physical backbone of AI—data centers, electrical grids, and water infrastructure—is critical national infrastructure that dictates sovereign capability. It aims to rapidly expand Scotland's "Green Compute" offering while rigorously managing the severe energy and environmental burdens inherently associated with hyperscale computing facilities.

### 2. Key Points

- Prioritises highly sustainable, "Green Compute" data centers that systematically leverage renewable energy and innovative heat-reuse systems.
- Actively seeks to collaborate with the UK Government and NESO to reform and accelerate notoriously slow electricity grid connection timescales.
- Commits to exploring 'Green AI-Ready' zoning designations and linking a dedicated AI Accelerator to the multi-billion Lanarkshire AI Growth Zone.
- Emphasizes the need for distributed compute architectures deployed over the national fibre backbone, not just hyperscale sites.

### 3. Observations

- **Feasibility** -- Achieving the stated goals for improving grid connection times requires high-level UK (NESO) cooperation, making this a pivotal external dependency that resides outside the direct legislative power of the Scottish Government.
- **Internal consistency** -- The strong, recurring emphasis on water resilience and heat reuse perfectly aligns with the Scottish Government's broader, pre-existing Net Zero commitments.
- **Gaps** -- While the text commits to ensuring "tangible benefits for local communities" hosting these massive facilities, it fails to specify what these benefits typically look like (e.g., guaranteed local employment quotas, civic funds, or discounted energy access).
