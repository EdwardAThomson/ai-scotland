### 1. Summary

This section introduces the central architectural model of the entire strategy: the "AI Stack". It organizes the government's programmatic response into six vertical technological layers, enveloped by two cross-cutting themes (Data and Regulation). It also establishes a phased schedule (updates in 2027 and 2029) to ensure the framework stays relevant against technological maturation.

### 2. Key Points

- Rejects monolithic approaches by adopting the "AI Stack" as a non-hierarchical frame of reference for organizing policy responses.
- Structurally divides interventions into six vertical capability layers (Users, Adoption & Skills, Companies & Products, Innovation & R&D, Data Centres & Infrastructure, Semiconductors).
- Envelops the stack within overarching "Data" and "Regulation" layers that impact all vertical tiers.
- Commits to a scheduled, three-phase delivery approach: Phase 1 (current), Phase 2 (2027), and Phase 3 (2029).

### 3. Observations

- **Clarity** -- The "AI Stack" paradigm smartly borrows from software engineering principles. While highly robust and resonant for digital professionals, it may require careful communication by the government to ensure politicians and lay audiences do not find the structural terminology alienating.
- **Internal consistency** -- Placing "Data" and "Regulation" as encircling rings is conceptually stellar, accurately reflecting that they are horizontal constraints that govern the success, safety, and functionality of every single vertical capability.
- **Measurability** -- The phased commitment to publish iterated actions in 2027 and 2029 builds a predictable rhythm for external auditors to assess longitudinal strategy execution.
