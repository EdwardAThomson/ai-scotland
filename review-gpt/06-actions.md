# Review: Actions

## Summary

This section translates the strategy into ten early actions to be completed by the end of March 2027. It is the first part of the document that gives a near-term delivery window and a concrete list of interventions spanning coordination, adoption, trust, health, skills, scaling, public services, infrastructure, and data access.

## Key Points

- Ten key actions are promised by the end of March 2027.
- `AI Scotland` is positioned as the flagship delivery vehicle for the strategy.
- Industry champions and an independent Expert Advisory Board will be appointed.
- A national engagement programme is promised to address public trust and confidence.
- A framework for safe, ethical, and efficient AI use in health and social care will be implemented.
- SME adoption support will be expanded through a revitalised national programme and AI Leadership Academy.
- A Future Jobs Panel will assess workforce impacts and support skills planning.
- New interventions are promised for scale-ups, public-service innovation, green data centres, the Lanarkshire AI Growth Zone, and public-sector data access.

## Observations

- **Clarity** -- The list is concrete enough to understand the government's intended direction, but most actions are still framed at a high level. Terms such as `position`, `launch`, `implement`, and `work with partners` do not yet make clear what will be built, who will own delivery, or how success will be judged.
- **Measurability** -- The presence of a deadline is a strength, but the actions mostly lack output measures. There are no targets for participation, investment, adoption, workforce reach, or public trust improvement, so even this more operational section leaves substantial room for loose interpretation.
- **Gaps** -- Several actions create new bodies, pilots, and programmes, but there is little on funding, sequencing, or delivery dependencies between them. The section also says little about procurement reform, capability inside government, or how the promised trust and ethics work will be enforced rather than merely encouraged.
- **Feasibility** -- Ten actions by March 2027 is ambitious, especially because many of them depend on coordination across government, industry, public services, and advisory bodies. The strategy may be able to launch these initiatives on time, but launching is not the same as making them effective.
- **Internal consistency** -- The action list covers most of the major themes established earlier in the strategy, including growth, skills, trust, public services, infrastructure, and data. However, Fair Work remains indirect rather than central, and the relationship between these short-term actions and the longer-term 2031 outcomes is still not fully spelled out.
