# Review: Layer 7 - Data

## Summary

This section presents public-sector data as a critical enabling layer for trustworthy AI, better services, research, and innovation. It argues that Scotland already has useful governance foundations but is being held back by fragmentation, slow access, uneven infrastructure, and limited interoperability.

## Key Points

- Scotland is said to have strong data governance foundations, but public-sector data is still not being used to its full value.
- The 2031 outcomes focus on coordinated public-sector data leadership, well-maintained data assets, AI-ready data, and secure access for research and innovation.
- The case for change identifies fragmentation, slow access processes, weak interoperability, and capability gaps as key barriers.
- The strategy links improved data-sharing to better services, faster research, and more trustworthy AI adoption.
- The 2027 actions include a data matchmaking pilot, barrier-mapping work, an AI public-service innovation programme, and a joint leadership group with local government.
- The NPL case study is used to emphasise trust, assurance, and measurable safety in AI systems.

## Observations

- **Clarity** -- The section clearly identifies data as an enabling constraint rather than an abstract asset. It recognises that AI capability depends not just on having data, but on being able to access, govern, reuse, and trust it across organisational boundaries.
- **Measurability** -- The outcomes are sensible in direction but still vague in execution. The strategy does not define what faster or easier access would mean in practice, how much interoperability improvement is needed, or how it will measure whether public-sector data has become genuinely `AI-ready`.
- **Gaps** -- The section says relatively little about consent, legitimacy, and public permission beyond broad references to trust and responsible use. It also leaves underdeveloped the question of institutional incentives: many public bodies do not share data because of legal caution, risk aversion, or capacity limits, and those behavioural barriers may matter as much as technical ones.
- **Feasibility** -- The proposed actions are plausible starting points, particularly the focus on barrier-mapping and joint leadership. But the distance between pilots and a mature, coordinated public-sector data ecosystem is large. Achieving that will likely require more than coordination; it will need persistent standards work, shared infrastructure, and strong governance authority.
- **Internal consistency** -- This section aligns well with the strategy's wider emphasis on trustworthy AI and better public services. It also supports several other layers at once. However, it exposes a recurring weakness in the strategy: the ambition is cross-cutting, while the delivery tools still look relatively incremental.
