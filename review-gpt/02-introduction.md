# Review: Introduction

## Summary

The introduction presents AI as both an economic opportunity and a disruptive force, and positions the strategy as Scotland's framework for responding to that moment responsibly. It introduces the AI Stack as the organising model for delivery and promises ongoing reporting, iteration, and collaboration as the technology evolves.

## Key Points

- The strategy aims to harness AI responsibly for sustainable growth and stronger public services.
- Scotland is positioned as needing to remain competitive in a fast-moving global AI landscape.
- The document sets out outcomes and actions intended to deliver value to people, places, and organisations across Scotland.
- The `AI Stack` is introduced as the core model through which action will be organised.
- The government commits to publishing progress reports and evolving the action plan over time.
- The strategy is presented as having been shaped through consultation with the Scottish Technology Council and several external partners.
- The quoted external voices emphasise Scotland's research strength, collaboration, and need for stronger international visibility.

## Observations

- **Clarity** -- The introduction is readable and direct, but it relies heavily on terms such as `responsibly`, `sustainable value`, and `delivered at scale` without defining them. The AI Stack is named but not yet explained enough for a new reader to understand why it is the chosen model.
- **Measurability** -- The promise of `regular reports on progress` is useful, but it lacks any detail about frequency, ownership, or what indicators will be tracked. That leaves a wide gap between the commitment to report and any practical accountability mechanism.
- **Gaps** -- The consultation story is narrower than the strategy's rhetoric suggests. The named contributors and quoted voices are concentrated in academia, business, and innovation bodies, with no equivalent visibility for trade unions, frontline public-service organisations, local government, or affected communities.
- **Feasibility** -- The section implies Scotland can keep pace with rapid global AI developments through an adaptive strategy process, but it does not yet explain what institutional capacity or decision-making structure will support that responsiveness.
- **Internal consistency** -- The introduction raises expectations that later sections will connect responsible AI, economic growth, public-service reform, and international competitiveness into one coherent programme. That is a high bar, and the rest of the strategy needs to show that these aims are mutually reinforcing rather than merely listed together.
