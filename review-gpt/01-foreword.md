# Review: Foreword

## Summary

The foreword frames AI as Scotland's next major opportunity to extend its historic tradition of scientific and technological leadership. It sets out a broad ambition to use AI for economic growth and better public services while insisting that adoption must be responsible, fair, and aligned with Scottish values.

## Key Points

- Scotland's history of innovation is presented as a foundation for leadership in AI.
- AI is described as a transformative economic opportunity that can drive productivity, innovation, and public-service improvement.
- The strategy is presented as a joint effort across government, industry, and academia.
- The government states that the benefits of AI should be secured for everyone in Scotland.
- Public concerns about transparency, fairness, jobs, and the pace of change are acknowledged.
- The approach is explicitly tied to the Scottish Government's commitment to Fair Work.
- The foreword argues that progress and safeguards should advance together.

## Observations

- **Clarity** -- The foreword is clear in tone and intent, but its core commitments are framed at a very high level. Phrases such as "secure the benefits of AI for everyone" and "lead instead of follow" are rhetorically strong but operationally vague.
- **Measurability** -- There are no concrete targets, dates, or delivery mechanisms here, which is normal for a foreword, but it does create a clear burden on later sections to define what success, fairness, and public benefit would actually look like in practice.
- **Gaps** -- The section acknowledges risks around jobs, transparency, and fairness, but says little about who bears these risks most directly or how trade-offs will be managed. It also says government, industry, and academia will work together, but does not mention civil society, trade unions, or local communities in that delivery framing.
- **Internal consistency** -- Fair Work is positioned as a central principle from the outset. That is useful framing, but it sets a standard the rest of the strategy needs to meet in concrete policy terms rather than leaving it as a values statement.
