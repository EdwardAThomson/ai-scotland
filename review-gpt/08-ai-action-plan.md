# Review: AI Action Plan

## Summary

This section introduces the `AI Stack`, the framework that structures the rest of the strategy. It argues that AI policy should be organised around interconnected layers spanning users, adoption, companies, research, infrastructure, semiconductors, data, and regulation, and it presents the action plan as a phased process running through 2027, 2029, and 2031.

## Key Points

- The Scottish Government presents the AI Stack as a new organising model for AI policy and delivery.
- The stack contains six named vertical layers: Users, AI Adoption and Skills, Companies and Products, Innovation and R&D, Data Centres and Infrastructure, and Semiconductors.
- Data and Regulation are treated as encircling layers that affect every other part of the stack.
- The layers are said to be non-hierarchical and highly interdependent.
- The AI Action Plan will proceed in three phases, with updates planned for 2027 and 2029.
- Phase 1 is the iteration set out in the current document.
- The strategy asserts a simple logic: if the actions and outcomes are delivered, the purpose will be achieved.

## Observations

- **Clarity** -- The section does a better job than the introduction of explaining what the AI Stack is, but it still leaves open why this model is superior to a more conventional policy structure. Calling it a `radical new approach` overstates how much explanatory work the section actually does.
- **Measurability** -- The phased structure is useful, but the section does not specify what each phase is expected to accomplish or how progress from one phase to the next will be assessed. Without phase-level criteria, the update timetable risks becoming procedural rather than substantive.
- **Gaps** -- The model is comprehensive in scope, but the section does not explain how prioritisation will work across the layers when resources or political attention are limited. It also does not address whether all layers are equally actionable within devolved competence, particularly where infrastructure, regulation, and industrial policy intersect with UK-wide powers.
- **Feasibility** -- The stack is a plausible organising device, especially for showing interdependence, but its breadth creates execution risk. A model that tries to hold the whole AI ecosystem at once can easily become too diffuse unless later sections establish strong priorities and trade-offs.
- **Internal consistency** -- The section helps reconcile the strategy's many moving parts by providing a single frame for the chapters that follow. Still, its closing claim that delivering the actions and outcomes will achieve the purpose is more asserted than demonstrated, because the causal links between the layers, the actions, and the headline outcomes remain only partly specified.
