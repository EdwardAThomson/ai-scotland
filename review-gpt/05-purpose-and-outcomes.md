# Review: Purpose & Outcomes

## Summary

This section condenses the strategy into a single purpose statement and eight outcomes grouped under four headings. It is intended to define what success should look like by 2031 and to provide a simple framework for interpreting the rest of the strategy.

## Key Points

- The strategy's purpose is to use AI to drive responsible and inclusive growth and to make a positive difference across society.
- The document groups its intended outcomes under `People and Skills`, `Companies and Innovation`, `Infrastructure`, and `Data and Regulation`.
- Two outcome statements are provided under each heading, creating eight headline outcomes in total.
- The section says these outcome statements represent `what success looks like` by the end of the strategy period in 2031.

## Observations

- **Clarity** -- The purpose statement is understandable, but the outcomes are framed at too high a level to provide much interpretive discipline. Terms such as `equality of access`, `strong technology clusters`, and `best practice` sound positive but leave substantial room for interpretation.
- **Measurability** -- This is the section's main weakness. None of the eight outcomes contains a baseline, target, indicator, or threshold. As written, they are not strong enough to serve as a serious test of success in 2031 because almost any partial progress could be presented as achievement.
- **Gaps** -- Important recurring themes from elsewhere in the strategy are underrepresented here. `Fair Work`, public trust, and environmental sustainability are all central to the narrative of the wider document, yet they do not appear explicitly as outcomes. That weakens the connection between the strategy's rhetoric and its chosen measures of success.
- **Internal consistency** -- The four headings are simpler than the later AI Stack structure, but the relationship between them is not made explicit. Readers are left to infer how the eight stack layers map onto these four outcome areas, which creates avoidable ambiguity in the strategy's logic.
- **Feasibility** -- The outcomes are broad enough that they do not yet force hard choices. That makes them politically safe, but it also means they provide limited guidance on prioritisation, resourcing, or what trade-offs the government is actually prepared to make to achieve them.
