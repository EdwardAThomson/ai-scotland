# Review: Endnotes

**Light review**

The endnotes provide a reasonably broad source base across Scottish Government documents, delivery bodies, university centres, and selected external frameworks. They are sufficient to show where many of the strategy's claims and policy influences come from.

## Notable observations

- **Several citations are thin.** Entries such as `According to data provided by Scottish Enterprise` do not give enough detail for a reader to verify the underlying claim independently.
- **There is some duplication.** The same `Artificial Intelligence -- Business Insights and Conditions in Scotland (wave 147)` source appears more than once.
- **The source base is heavily domestic.** That fits a national strategy, but it means the document cites relatively little comparative international evidence beyond OECD principles, the EU AI Act, and one McKinsey reference.
- **The markdown file includes publication metadata after the endnotes.** That appears to be carryover from the PDF's final publication page rather than part of the endnotes section itself.
