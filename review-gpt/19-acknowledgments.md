# Review: Acknowledgments

**Light review**

The section makes clear that the strategy was informed by consultation across business, academia, innovation bodies, and support agencies, and it names the six members of the Scottish Technology Council AI Sub-group that guided the work.

## Notable observations

- **The named leadership group is narrow.** For a national AI strategy, six named advisers is a small core, and the group is weighted toward academia and technology businesses.
- **Public-service representation is not visible in the named list.** That is notable given the strategy's repeated emphasis on transforming public services.
- **Trade unions and wider civil society are not named.** That sits awkwardly with the strategy's stated concern for Fair Work, workforce impacts, and public trust.
- **The section signals breadth of engagement but limited transparency on who shaped decisions.** Many contributors are described in aggregate rather than named, which makes it harder to assess whose perspectives carried most weight.
