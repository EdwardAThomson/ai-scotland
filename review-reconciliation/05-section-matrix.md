## Cross-Cutting Patterns

| Pattern | Frequency | Detail |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| **Outcomes lack measurable targets** | 3/3 reviewers, across nearly every section | The single most consistent finding. All three reviewers independently and repeatedly flag that outcomes have no baselines, metrics, thresholds, or KPIs. |
| **Fair Work is promised but not embedded** | 3/3 reviewers | Introduced in the foreword as central, but only meaningfully integrated in Layer 2 (Adoption & Skills). Missing from outcomes, most layer actions, and the acknowledgments group. |
| **No budgets or funding commitments** | 3/3 reviewers | Not a single action across the entire strategy includes a cost, funding source, or resource allocation. |
| **Consultation base is narrow** | 3/3 reviewers (Claude/GPT strongly; Gemini mildly) | Named contributors are concentrated in tech/academia. Trade unions, local government, healthcare, and civil society are absent from named roles despite being stakeholders in the strategy's own framing. |
| **Foundations section is promotional** | 2/3 reviewers (Claude/GPT) | Claude and GPT both note the section reads as an investment pitch with no weaknesses analysis. Gemini takes a more positive view. |
| **Actions create structures, not results** | 3/3 reviewers | Most actions are "launch", "establish", "pilot", or "explore". They create panels, programmes, and pilots but do not commit to measurable delivery. |
| **Devolved power limits regulatory ambition** | 3/3 reviewers | All three note that Scotland's regulatory aspirations (especially EU AI Act alignment) are constrained by UK-level decision-making, making the regulation layer advocacy rather than delivery. |
| **Risk register is incomplete** | 3/3 reviewers | All three independently flag that algorithmic bias is limited to gender, omitting race, disability, and socioeconomic factors. Claude also flags missing risks around deepfakes, cybersecurity, and strategy execution. |
| **Gemini is consistently more positive** | Observable across all sections | Gemini's reviews are notably less critical than Claude or GPT, tending to emphasise strengths and feasibility rather than gaps and weaknesses. This is a methodological difference rather than a factual disagreement. |
| **Claude provides the most granular critique** | Observable across all sections | Claude's reviews are the longest and most detailed, frequently identifying specific numerical inconsistencies, case study mismatches, and cross-reference failures. |
| **GPT occupies the analytical middle ground** | Observable across all sections | GPT's reviews balance critique and acknowledgment, consistently framing findings around governance, feasibility, and institutional capacity. |
